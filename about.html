I'm Ida Skovmand, a writer, sometimes photographer, sometimes coffee-shop creator.

I like flan, hunting for public domain images, braiding bread and finding stray playing cards on the street.

My writing (this link leads to a tumblr-era portfolio) has appeared in publications such as Tablet Magazine, The Skirt Chronicles, Lindsay Magazine, I love you magazine and more. I've ghostwritten a blog for an opera and shot the pictures for a French cookbook. Between 2006 and 2024, I wrote regularly for Swedish news media about culture, mostly music.

As debillie, my partner Aviv and I do big and small mixed media projects around food. Events, zines, f&b concepts and art direction. We used to run a café. One day when the stars align we’ll do it again.

I’m currently working on Pamphlette, a publishing project. This suddenly makes sense after a long time of confused scurrying between two separate piles: texts and images. Now all that is jumbled together on the same desk. More on that soon.

I was born in the south of Sweden, to Danish and Norwegian parents. After dwelling in Copenhagen, London, Paris and Jaffa, I’m currently based in Vienna.
